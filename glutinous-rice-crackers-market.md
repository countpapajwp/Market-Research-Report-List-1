<p><strong>The Glutinous Rice crackers Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Arare</li><li>Okaki</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Glutinous rice crackers, primarily Arare and Okaki, serve distinct niches in the market. Arare are small, bite-sized snacks, often seasoned or flavored, and commonly enjoyed as a crunchy treat. They are typically made by puffing or frying glutinous rice, resulting in a light texture. In contrast, Okaki are larger, thicker crackers, traditionally made by baking or grilling sticky rice cakes, offering a heartier and chewier experience. Both types cater to diverse consumer preferences, appealing to those seeking unique, gluten-free snack options.</p></p>
